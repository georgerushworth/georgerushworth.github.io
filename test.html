CC3: Debating

Task: Produce two charts that support or refute a topic discussed at the Festival of Economics.

Proposition: Governments should prioritize growth-oriented tax cuts even when unfunded, as economic expansion will generate offsetting revenues.

I'll use Liz Truss's controversial 2022 mini-budget as a case study to examine this claim. The mini-budget included Â£45 billion in unfunded tax cuts, triggering a severe market reaction.

Evidence #1: Interest Rate Response

The Bank of England was forced to raise rates aggressively following the mini-budget announcement. Rather than stimulating growth, the unfunded tax cuts fueled inflation fears, leading to monetary tightening that constrained economic activity.

Evidence #2: Bond Market Turmoil

Perhaps more dramatically, gilt yields spiked immediately after the mini-budget, forcing pension funds to sell assets and ultimately requiring Bank of England intervention. The market's verdict was clear: unfunded tax cuts damaged fiscal credibility rather than generating growth-driven revenues.

Both charts refute the proposition. Truss's experiment demonstrated that unfunded tax cuts can destabilize markets and force contractionary policy responses, the opposite of the intended growth effect.
