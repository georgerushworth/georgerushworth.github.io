Background

Productivity, output per hour worked, is the primary driver of wages and living standards. An S&S ISA allows UK residents to invest up to £20,000 annually in stocks, bonds, and funds, with all returns tax-free. Unlike a Cash ISA, which functions like a savings account, an S&S ISA exposes savings to capital markets, offering higher long-term returns in exchange for some investment risk.

Chronic Underinvestment Has Stalled UK Productivity Since 2008

UK productivity has barely grown since the financial crisis. As Chart 1 shows, output per hour worked has risen only around 5% since 2008, against a pre-crisis trend that would have delivered nearly 40% growth. Annual productivity growth averaged just 0.5% from 2010 to 2022, placing the UK well behind France, Germany, and the United States (van Ark and O'Mahony, 2024). If this continues, the UK cannot sustain current living standards.
